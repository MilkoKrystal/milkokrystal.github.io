Hearing is Believing

  • We form beliefs without vetting most of them, and maintain them even after receiving clear, corrective information.
  • Truthseeking, the desire to know the truth regardless of wether the truth aligns with the beliefs we currently hold, is not naturally supported by the way we process information. We might think of ourselves as open-minded and capable of updating our beliefs based on new information, but the research conclusively shows otherwise. Instead of altering our beliefs to fit new information, we do the opposite, altering our interpretation of that information to fit our beliefs.

    Redefining Confidence

  • We can express how confident we are by thinking about the number of plausible alternatives and declaring that range.
